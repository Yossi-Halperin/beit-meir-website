# Photography Brief — Beit Meir Website
## Phase 2 — Real Photography

> **Status:** All images in the current site are AI-generated placeholders.
> Real photography is required before public launch.

---

## Art Direction

**Color grading:** Warm, slightly desaturated, golden cast. All photos must look like they belong to one campaign — as if shot by the same photographer on the same day, in the same light.

**Mood:** Quiet luxury. Restraint. Not "look at this amazing property" but "this is a place that has existed for 70 years and will exist for 70 more."

**Avoid:** Generic stock mansion photos, staged interiors, anything that looks like a real estate brochure.

---

## Required Shots (12–15 total)

### Hero / Full-bleed (2–3 shots)
- Aerial/drone: Beit Meir from above at golden hour (late afternoon, ~1 hour before sunset). Slowly approaching the pine forest. The moshav should be visible — houses, trees, the road.
- Wide landscape: The Judean Hills from the moshav, looking toward Jerusalem. Pine forest in foreground, hills in distance, warm haze.
- Aerial: The moshav at dusk/blue hour, lights beginning to appear.

### Gallery / Editorial (5–7 shots)
- Close-up: Jerusalem stone wall or gate, warm afternoon light, slight texture detail
- Forest path: Walking trail through the 170-year-old Jerusalem pines — dappled light, no people
- Vineyard: Rows of vines on the hillside, golden hour, the hills in the background
- Shabbat table: Set for Shabbat, viewed from behind or from the side — not staged, not stock. Candles, challah, white tablecloth. Warm interior light.
- Family: Older couple walking on a forest path, viewed from behind. No faces needed.
- Children: Kids on a path through the pines, natural and unposed.
- View from a plot: Looking out from a building site toward the forest/hills.

### Location / Context (2–3 shots)
- The Burma Road marker or sign
- Entrance to the moshav — the gate, the road, the pines
- View toward Jerusalem from the moshav (the city visible in the distance)
